# Context: Why an Air Quality Dashboard for Schools?

Children spend a significant proportion of their waking lives in educational buildings, with evidence suggesting around 30% of waking hours are spent at school. As a result, the quality of the indoor environment—particularly (IAQ) and thermal conditions—plays a critical role in children’s health, wellbeing, attendance, and learning outcomes [1](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3298/12/8/261).

A growing body of scientific evidence shows that classrooms frequently contain elevated levels of air pollutants, including particulate matter (PM₂.₅ and PM₁₀), nitrogen dioxide (NO₂), volatile organic compounds (VOCs), and biological contaminants such as mould. Many of these pollutants originate outdoors, particularly from road traffic and nearby industry, and infiltrate indoors even in relatively airtight buildings [1](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3298/12/8/261). Poor ventilation, often indicated by high carbon dioxide (CO₂) levels, exacerbates this problem and is common in schools across many countries, including the UK.

Children are particularly vulnerable to these exposures due to their developing lungs and brains, higher breathing rates relative to body size, and limited control over their environment. The evidence reviewed shows consistent links between poor IAQ and increased respiratory symptoms, illness-related school absence, and reduced concentration and cognitive performance. Indirect impacts—such as missed school days leading to lower educational attainment—are especially important at a population level [1](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3298/12/8/261).

Despite these risks, the review highlights that effective interventions are often practical and affordable. Improving the operation of existing ventilation systems, controlling pollution sources, and deploying targeted air cleaning can deliver measurable benefits at relatively low cost.

This dashboard is designed to translate this complex scientific evidence into clear, actionable information for parents, using AI tools to synthesise data, highlight risk, and support informed engagement with schools about children’s learning environments.
